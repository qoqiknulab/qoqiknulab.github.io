Research

Quantum Information Theory

We investigate the theoretical foundations and practical applications of quantum information, exploring how quantum systems can be harnessed for computation, communication, and measurement beyond classical limits.

Quantum Metrology

We study how quantum resources such as entanglement and squeezing can enhance measurement precision beyond the standard quantum limit. Our work involves quantum Fisher information, Cramér–Rao bounds, and optimal estimation strategies for physical parameters.

Quantum Communication

Our research addresses the theoretical aspects of quantum key distribution (QKD), quantum teleportation, and quantum networks. We analyze channel capacities and develop security proofs for quantum cryptographic protocols.

Quantum Computing

We explore quantum algorithms, quantum error correction codes, and the fundamental computational advantages offered by quantum systems, with a focus on near-term and fault-tolerant quantum architectures.

Quantum Optics Theory

We investigate the theoretical foundations of quantum optics, including light–matter interactions at the quantum level, nonclassical states of light, photon statistics, coherence theory, and cavity quantum electrodynamics (QED). [연구 내용 상세 설명을 추가해 주세요.]
